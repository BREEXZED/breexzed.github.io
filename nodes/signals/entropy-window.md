Entropy keeps returning to me as a signal that reality never fully agrees to remain organized for long. It appears whenever systems fray at the edges: in rooms that drift toward disorder, in moods that resist neat naming, and in structures that reveal their cost the moment maintenance stops.

I keep this signal because it helps me read decay without treating decay as failure. Sometimes entropy is loss; sometimes it is exposure. Either way, it tells the truth about what a thing demands in order to keep existing.
